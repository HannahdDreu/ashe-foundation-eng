A clothing store for Naserian


I met Naserian (24 years old and mother of 3 young children) during my first visit to the Maasai village in Loolera. Despite Naserian's incredibly warm welcome, she left a disheartened impression on me. It's not surprising, as in 2022, Naserian divorced due to her ex-husband's alcohol abuse and domestic violence. As a result, she now lives with her three children at her mother's place. It's a distressing situation, leaving Naserian with little future prospects without a husband.

Early last year, Naserian asked me for help. She expressed her desire to sell sugar in Loolera to generate a modest income for herself and her three children. She needed 100 euros to buy enough sugar to start a small sugar trade. Naserian seemed like a sensible young woman eager to work her way out of the hopeless situation. I wanted to help her. So, at that time, I donated 100 euros to Naserian for the establishment of her sugar trade. Now, about a year and a half later, Naserian is still selling sugar and has created a small source of income for herself and her children. This allows her to provide for the basic needs of herself and her children.

Donating 100 euros was a small effort for me, but it had a big impact on Naserian's life. However, the future for her and her three children remains very uncertain. To offer her children a better future and being as entrepreneurial as she is, Naserian told me during my second visit last year that she - in addition to her sugar trade - would like to start her own clothing store in the center of Loolera. There's no clothing store in the center of Loolera yet, so the Maasai in Loolera and the surrounding area have to travel 4 hours by bus to buy clothes.

With this clothing store, Naserian can create a sustainable source of income, build livelihood security, and provide her children with a future. She told me that with this income, she wants to send her children to school so they can have a better future through education. All Naserian needs to set up the store are the financial resources. She asked me to help her with this. Naserian needs 5,000 euros in start-up capital to set up her clothing store. Naserian is a reliable, intelligent, and decisive young woman who makes sensible choices. Ashe Foundation would like to support her in setting up the clothing store and asks for your help.
