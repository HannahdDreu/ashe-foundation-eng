Who we are

Board members and our local partner

The board of Ashe Foundation consists of three members and they are jointly authorized.
No financial benefits are provided to the directors of Ashe Foundation.

All financial donations go directly to the Maasai community.



Amory Berkenveld

Amory is the founder and chairperson of Ashe Foundation. She resides in both the Netherlands and Tanzania.

As an academic in public administration with extensive experience in the public domain, she is passionate about contributing to a fairer (international) society. Her intrinsic motivation to make the world more socioeconomically equitable has led to the establishment of Ashe Foundation. Amory primarily performs the duties as an executive director from Tanzania.

"With my intrinsic values and motivation to help people, and my conviction that everyone should have the opportunity to develop themselves in life in a fair and just manner, I founded Ashe Foundation with much love and joy last year."

Roxane Root


Roxane is a board member and secretary of Ashe Foundation.

With a background in secretarial work, Roxane Root will serve as the Secretary within Ashe Foundation. She will provide support during meetings, where projects to be undertaken will be discussed and board decisions will be made. These meetings will be documented, and Roxane will undertake the role of secretary.

"I want to make a difference in the world. Working at Ashe Foundation is a direct way to have a positive impact on the lives of people in Tanzania. By sharing my time and talents, I can help improve the living conditions of children and others and give them a better future. It gives me a sense of satisfaction and purpose and helps enrich my own life."

Hannah de Dreu


Hannah is a board member and treasurer of Ashe Foundation.

As the treasurer of Ashe Foundation, she utilizes her organizational talents to create positive impact where it is most needed. Her travel experiences have taught her that real change begins with understanding and respecting different cultures, with the aim of empowering people to regain control over their own lives.

"Change is not always a leap into the unknown, but often a journey of small steps that together make a big impact. With Ashe Foundation, we hope to have a positive impact on the Maasai community."




Kangai Oropi

Kangai was born in Tanzania. He is the eldest son in the Maasai family and therefore also takes care of his family. He has been working as a Maasai security guard for tourists since 2015.

As a local partner of Ashe Foundation, Kangai is an important link because he is part of the community.

He serves as a volunteer for Ashe Foundation in his role as a local partner.
